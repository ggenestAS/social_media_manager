# EXP-2026-06-22-exam-automatismes

> Supersedes `EXP-2026-06-22-outcome-hook` (that one led with positioning + CTA —
> an ad in a notebook skin, all carousels). This one is **value-first** and
> **reels-first**.

**Hypothesis:** A post that *teaches an automatisme* and anchors it to the exact
spé/bac moment where it costs points (« le calcul caché dans une dérivée »,
« sur un arbre de probas tu multiplies ») will outperform both our context-free
drills (no stakes, no lesson) and our institutional posts (no value), because the
student leaves with something usable. The brand benefit is *earned* by the value,
never led with. We expect this to travel best as **reels** — the format we ship
~10×/day and most need a winning template for.

**Deviation axis:** content angle (teach + exam-anchor vs bare drill) × modality
(reels-first, not the static-carousel default I wrongly used last time).

**Breaks from:**
- `calcul-du-jour` / `reel` — they pose a naked calc and reveal only the answer.
  Here the reveal **teaches the method** and the hook names the **exam chapter**.
- `astuce` — teaches a method, but generic and context-free; never tied to a
  named spé/bac task, and the close is "save/follow", not value-then-soft-CTA.
- the institutional `outcome-hook` drafts — those *sold* the outcome; these
  *deliver* the training that produces it.

**Scope guardrail (chosen — option A):** Math stays **mental calculation** (the
product wedge). We break only the content guide's "no exam framing" rule on the
*framing*: each calc is the head-doable atom hiding inside a real exam task, not
a full exam problem. No dérivée walkthroughs, no factorisation — just the fast
sub-skill. Every result recomputed and verified.

**What "worked" would look like:** saves ("I'll drill this"), comments stating
their solve time, shares to study-group DMs, and "do more like this" replies —
judged by eye. The read that matters: save rate of a value-reel vs a plain
`reel`, since the bet is teaching > testing.

**Platform(s):** reels → ig + tiktok (9:16); carousel + post → ig + fb (4:5).
