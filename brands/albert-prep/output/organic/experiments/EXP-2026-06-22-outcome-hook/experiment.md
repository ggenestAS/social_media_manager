# EXP-2026-06-22-outcome-hook

**Hypothesis:** An outcome-first hook — leading with the student's *goal/identity*
(« Prépare ton Bac de maths », « De 10 à 20 », « Prépare ta Spé ») instead of a
bare calculation — will beat the brand's number-first formats on stop-rate and
saves among Première/Terminale, because it sells the destination (a grade) the
audience already searches for before asking them to do mental maths, and reframes
the daily drill as the shortest path there.

**Deviation axis:** Hook style + content angle (goal/identity hook vs « the number
is the hook »), with a new structural arc.

**Breaks from:** *Every* existing post-type leads with the math itself —
`calcul-du-jour` (bare calc), `astuce` (method question + teaser calc), `serie`
(family + timer), `reel` (calc + countdown), `qcm` (question + 3 options). None
opens on the student's outcome/level. These drafts invert that: slide 1 is the
**destination**, the calc only appears on slide 3 as *proof* of the path. The arc
is also new — **Objectif → la faille (le wedge) → preuve (drill) → CTA lié à
l'objectif** — not the astuce teach-arc nor the serie quiz-arc.

**Scope guardrail (deliberate):** The seeds are exam/grade framing, which the
content guide bans *on the math*. We break it only on the **hook/positioning**;
the drilled calculations stay general CORE-calibrated mental maths and every
answer is verified. No "question Bac", no exam-specific math — just exam-level
*motivation* wrapped around fluency reps.

**What "worked" would look like:** saves (the "I'll use this to prep" signal),
sends/DMs, comments stating a current grade or target ("je suis à 13, j'veux 17"),
and diagnostic-link clicks — judged by eye, no formal stats. The save rate vs a
matched `astuce` is the cleanest read.

**Platform(s):** ig (feed carousel) + fb cross-post. 4:5, 1080×1350, renderable.

## Drafts

All three execute the one hypothesis; the probed sub-variable is **which goal
anchor** converts the same general-fluency drill.

1. `01-prepare-ton-bac` — exam-outcome anchor (Terminale, Bac year) — renderable
2. `02-de-10-a-20` — grade-transformation anchor (the 10→20 jump) — renderable
3. `03-prepare-ta-spe` — level-transition anchor (Première entering Spé, pace fear) — renderable

### Seeds deliberately dropped
- **« prépare tes maths de seconde »** — Seconde sits outside the stated audience
  (Première/Terminale). It widens the funnel but dilutes the wedge; test the core
  segments first, add Seconde only if an anchor wins.
- **« révise tes maths »** — too generic to be a hook; it's the category, not a
  destination. Folded into the body copy, not used as a cover.
